Antihistamines - anti-allergy
Mechanism of action
What is Histamine?
A substance that is secreted after identifying an allergen and triggers an allergic immune response like Increased secretion in the mucous membranes, dilation of blood vessels (redness and edema), constriction of bronchi, etc.
What are Antihistamines?
Medicines that block the receptors for histamine and therefore inhibit its activity (inhibit and suppress allergy symptoms).
Loratadine, Desloratadine
Azelastine (Sol.)
Steroids - immune response suppressors
Mechanism of action
What are Steroids?
Hormones that are secreted from the adrenal gland and play many roles in the body such as: blood pressure balance, sugar level balance and more..
In addition, steroids suppress the activity of the immune system.
In what situations are steroid drugs given? Allergic reactions, inflammatory diseases, hormonal deficiencies and more...

Hypertension
Mechanism of action
"Diuretic" drugs - drugs that increase the excretion of water and salts in the urine → reduction of blood volume → decrease in blood pressure.
Medicines that slow down the heart rate and/or weaken the heartbeat → decrease in cardiac output → decrease in blood pressure.
Medicines that dilate blood vessels (relax muscle in the blood vessel walls) → reduced resistance in the blood vessels → decrease in blood pressure.

Reducing the absorption of cholesterol from the intestines into the blood.
